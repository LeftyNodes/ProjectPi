Energy Usage Per Pi

Assuming a power consumption of 15 Watts
and operating 24 hours a day, every day,
the energy consumption can be calculated as follows:

E(kWh/day) = P(W) × t(h/day) / 1000(W/kW)
E(kWh/day) = 15(W) × 24(h/day) / 1000(W/kW) = 0.360(kWh/day)

...And the cost of running the Raspberry Pi
node can be calculated as follows:

Cost($/day) = E(kWh/day) × Cost(cent/kWh) / 100(cent/$)
Cost($/day) = 0.360(kWh/day) × 14(cent/kWh) / 100(cent/$) = $0.0504($/day)

So, at 14 cents per kilowatt hour,
the daily cost of running a Raspberry Pi
node would be approximately $0.0504, or
$1.512 per month, and $18.396 per year.
